# Mental Model — Restaurant Network Analogy

## Purpose

Provides an intuitive way to understand the system using a **restaurant network analogy**.

---

## One-Line Summary

A supply chain is like a **network of restaurants managing ingredients, demand, and operations under uncertainty**.

---

## The Restaurant Network

Imagine operating **100 restaurants across the country**.

Each restaurant must answer:

- how many meals will be ordered  
- how much inventory is needed  
- when to reorder ingredients  
- how to share inventory across locations  
- how to respond to disruptions  

Retail supply chains operate the same way.

---

## Mapping the System

| Supply Chain | Restaurant |
|-------------|-----------|
| SKU | Ingredient |
| Store | Restaurant |
| Warehouse | Central kitchen |
| Demand | Customer orders |
| Forecast | Predicting orders |
| Inventory | Ingredients in fridge |
| Replenishment | Ordering ingredients |
| Allocation | Sending ingredients |
| Disruption | Supplier delay |
| Stockout | Running out of food |
| Service Level | Orders fulfilled |

---

## Demand Layer (ML)

Question:

What will customers order?

Examples:

- predict meals tomorrow  
- estimate demand spikes  
- model promotions  

**Mental Hook:** ML predicts what customers want

---

## Inventory Layer

Question:

What do we currently have?

Examples:

- ingredient levels  
- days of supply  
- risk of running out  

**Mental Hook:** inventory = current reality

---

## Decision Layer (Replenishment)

Question:

What should we do?

Examples:

- reorder ingredients  
- adjust quantities  
- maintain buffers  

**Mental Hook:** decisions turn signals into actions

---

## Simulation Layer

Question:

What happens if conditions change?

Examples:

- sudden demand increase  
- supplier delay  
- inventory loss  

**Mental Hook:** simulation = testing lab

---

## Disruption Layer

Question:

How does the system react to real-world failures?

Examples:

- delivery delays  
- supply shortages  
- operational issues  

**Mental Hook:** disruption = stress on the system

---

## Allocation Layer

Question:

Who gets inventory when supply is limited?

Examples:

- multiple restaurants need same ingredients  
- limited stock available  

**Mental Hook:** allocation = distributing scarcity

---

## Monitoring Layer

Question:

Is the system healthy?

Examples:

- stockout risk  
- service levels  
- performance metrics  

**Mental Hook:** monitoring = control tower

---

## LLM Layer

Question:

How do we explain decisions?

Examples:

- summarize risks  
- explain outcomes  
- compare scenarios  

**Mental Hook:** LLM = decision copilot

---

## System Goal

Combine:

- machine learning  
- operational logic  
- simulation

- allocation  
- monitoring  
- reasoning  

into a single system.
